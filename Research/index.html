Second Undergraduate Thesis (2022-2023)

Detecting Stellar Stream Exoplanets through the Twinkle Satellite.

Trottier Summer Internship at the Institute for Research on Exoplanets (2022)

This position was under the supervision of Professor Jonathan Gagné. Working jointly at the Institute for Research on Exoplanets at the Université de Montréal and Planétarium Rio Tinto Alcan, this project primarily focused on the identification of astrometric exoplanet candidates in Gaia Data Release 3 (DR3). Sorting and filtering many properties of stars in the data, Daniella identified 284 candidate exoplanet host stars, 34 of which are young stars that were then sent for direct imaging.

First Undergraduate Thesis (2021-2022)

The Hubble eXtreme Deep Field. Credit: NASA, ESA, G. Illingworth, D. Magee, and P. Oesch (University of California, Santa Cruz), R. Bouwens (Leiden University), and the HUDF09 Team.

In a continuation of the work done in the Summer Undergraduate Research Program (SURP) under the supervision of Dr. Lamiya Mowla and Dr. Kartheik Iyer, Daniella used the pipeline that was developped in summer 2021 to measure the morphology of galaxies in the Hubble eXtreme Deep Field (XDF). Measuring the half-light and half-mass radii - that is, the circular apertures containing half the image's flux - of galaxies reveals characteristics of morphological poperties such as stellar mass and specific star formation rate. These measurements were found for the XDF galaxies which were larger than 100 pixels on the field and had redshift between 0.5 and 6. This work is vital to understanding and predicting both galaxy morphology and evolution.
